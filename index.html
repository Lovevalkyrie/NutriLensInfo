AI-powered smart glasses for nutrition guidance

Turn everyday meals into moments of clarity.

NutriLens is a wearable food-analysis system that captures what a user is about to eat, transfers the image to a mobile app, analyzes nutritional content in real time, and can mirror the result back to a wearable display.

The goal is simple: make nutrition feedback more immediate, more accessible, and less dependent on manual logging or technical health literacy.

Purpose

Built around a real problem, not just a technical demo.

The problem

Food insecurity is often described as a lack of consistent access to food, but an equally important issue is the lack of nutritional understanding among vulnerable populations. During SNAP funding disruptions, many underserved families were left uncertain not only about securing meals, but also about whether those meals supported their health needs.

Existing tools like calorie-tracking apps, printed guides, and nutrition websites often require stable internet, time, and higher health literacy than many users possess. The gap is not just food access. It is the absence of simple, real-time nutrition guidance when decisions are actually made.

The response

NutriLens is an AI-powered smart-glasses system that provides real-time nutritional information through automated food recognition. When a user looks at a meal, the built-in camera captures visual data, the model identifies foods and estimates portions, and the system generates key nutritional values through a connected mobile application.

Instead of requiring manual scanning or typing, NutriLens is designed to provide practical guidance through a more natural, hands-light workflow.

How It Works

From meal capture to mobile analysis to wearable feedback.

01

Capture

A camera mounted on the glasses frame captures the food image from the user's point of view.

02

Transfer

The Raspberry Pi sends the image to the iPhone app over Bluetooth Low Energy.

03

Analyze

Gemini estimates food items, calories, protein, carbohydrates, fat, and a short health summary.

04

Reflect

The app displays the results, saves history for signed-in users, and can mirror the latest nutrition stats back to the wearable HUD.

Wearable Hardware

The prototype was built to be worn and tested, not just described.

Prototype components

  • camera mounted to the glasses frame
  • Raspberry Pi-based processing unit
  • physical control interface for interaction
  • BLE communication with the mobile app
  • optional heads-up display mirroring path

Mobile App

The iPhone and iPad app is where the image becomes usable guidance.

BLE connection flow

Users connect the phone to the Raspberry Pi, receive captured images, and manage the hardware workflow from the app.

AI nutrition output

Each analysis includes detected food, calorie estimate, macros, and a short summary designed to be easier to understand than a raw nutrition label.

History and account support

Signed-in users can save prior analyses, revisit them later, and manage account access directly in the app.

Why the system matters

Traditional nutrition apps often depend on manual meal entry and numerical interpretation, which creates friction and discourages consistent use. By recognizing food automatically through the user’s field of vision, NutriLens is designed to make feedback more immediate and more usable at the moment it matters.

The longer-term goal is not just convenience. It is to support healthier decisions through accessible, everyday nutritional awareness.
